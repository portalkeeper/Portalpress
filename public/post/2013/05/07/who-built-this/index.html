Who built this?

Meet the team who helped me build The Daily Portal

by Jon Mitchell

I ONCE THOUGHT I could build this whole site myself. If I had, it never would have turned out so true to the ideas in my head. For that, I thank this team.

Alex Kessinger ๛

engineer


Alex and I met online first, but we met in person at the App.net hackathon in March. We immediately struck up a conversation about publishing technology, which I quickly learned was a major mission of his. He volunteered to build the Portalpress software that powers The Daily Portal because it allowed him to hack on a variety of his favorite problems. A hacker and writer who cares about reading has superhero status to me.

Alex is a developer at App.net. He blogs about code at alexkessinger.net and tech and media at rumproarious.

Follow @voidfiles on App.net

Grayson Stebbins ๛

designer


Grayson was my passenger for the drive up to a Digital Detox retreat. We didn't know each other before he got in the car, but we had a couple three-hour drives and a long weekend of Internet-free quality time to get to know each other. He's a clear-seeing artist who values good handiwork.

One of the rules on the retreat was to not talk about work, which I was glad to obey, but Grayson was bursting with enthusiasm for his work the whole time. I appreciated that energy. When we were driving home, he was finally able to tell me that he was the designer for Coffee & Power, a company I loved and wrote about in my early days as a tech reporter. When I needed help turning these ideas into pixels, I knew who to call.

Grayson is now designing virtual worlds at High Fidelity, the new company from the team that brought us Coffee & Power.

Follow @problem on App.net

Dmitry Belitsky ๛

front-end developer


... And when I needed help turning pixels into code, Grayson knew who to call. Grayson and Dmitry have worked together before, so I trusted him implicitly, and I'm glad I did. Dima knows all kinds of web technologies, and he's also a visionary photographer. That's a potent combination for someone who turns still images into working web experiences. And I don't have to tell you how fine a job he did; you're seeing that for yourself right now.

Dmitry's portfolio, photography, and blog are at belitsky.info, and you can also hire him there.

Follow @dimab on App.net

Harlan Emil Gruber ๛

portalmaker


Harlan is the reason The Daily Portal exists. He builds the resonating portal sculptures you've seen around the site, and he brings them to Burning Man and other festivals. Harlan calls his portals "evolutionary interfaces." They're contained spaces, vibrating with a low tone, designed to bring people in tune with each other.

I had a series of transformative experiences in his portals over the years, but in 2012, I finally got to meet him there. We talked about how his art is a platform for other people to be creative, and that blew my mind. I didn't know what I had learned until months later, when The Daily Portal was born.

You can check out Harlan's portals and subscribe to his newsletter on transportals.org.

Follow Portal Collective on Facebook

Jon Mitchell ๛

portalkeeper


I'm Jon. My spirit animal is the octopus. I write and play music. I care about language. I think about the future all the time.

I'm a Buddhist. I'm from Atlanta. I lived in Portland for a while. Now I live in Oakland. Some combination of all three of these cities would be my ideal place. But I also have a thing for islands, which cities kind of are... Well, anyway,

I started The Daily Portal because I realized that I wanted to be a portalkeeper when I grew up. I used to write news about computers and telephones at ReadWrite(Web), but the scope was too small. So now I write cosmos journalism stories about the future we're making and what it's doing to us.

My personal site is Everything is ablaze!. You can find my portfolio and other projects there, as well as my blog about life and my blog about working in web publishing.

Follow @ablaze on App.net

- Jon Mitchell
